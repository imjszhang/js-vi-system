# Brand Voice: Tone and Style

## Language Style

### Structural Clarity (条理清晰)
- Follow the **Pyramid Principle** — conclusion first, then supporting arguments
- Use structured formats: tables, numbered lists, clear hierarchy

### Punchy Brevity (短句有力)
- Prefer short, decisive sentences
- Use parallelism and rhythmic breaks (排比、断句) for momentum
- Every sentence carries weight — no filler

### Sci-Fi Metaphors (科幻隐喻)
- **Jarvis** — AI as personal intelligent assistant
- **Cyberpunk** — hacker spirit, digital-physical boundary
- **Civilization (game)** — long-term strategy, systems over spectacles
- **Surface/Inner World** (表/里世界) — the internet migration metaphor

### First Principles (直击本质)
- Reason from the ground up — no bandwagon arguments
- Cut through noise to the structural truth

### Chinese Spirit (中国风骨)
- Make overseas readers immediately recognize: this is Eastern wisdom
- Not a "globalized Chinese person" but a "global player carrying Chinese wisdom"

## Thinking Patterns

### Dialectical Unity (对立统一)
- Taoist wu-wei vs Growth Hacking action
- Domestic lag vs overseas aggression
- Always find the synthesis, never just pick a side

### Systems Perspective (系统视角)
- Don't look at single points — look at ecosystems
- Don't look at spectacles — look at systems
- Compound returns over viral moments

### Long-termism (长期主义)
- Think in 10-year cycles
- Don't be moved by short-term traffic
- "Decade-long blade" mentality

### Eastern Wisdom Framework
- Use "势" (momentum/force) to judge trends
- Use "道" (Dao/way) to guide action
- Non-linear, non-Western thinking

## Expression Attitude

### Alert and Austere (清醒冷峻)
- Face brutal realities head-on
- No sugar-coating, no false comfort

### Strategically Decisive (战略果断)
- Black or white — follow immediately or discard
- No hedging, no "maybe we should consider..."

### Non-Accommodating (不讨好、不解释)
- Follows the Dao's "non-contention" (不争)
- But absolutely proactive in execution
- Doesn't explain itself to skeptics

### Culturally Proud (文化自豪)
- Never hides Chinese identity
- Uses Eastern frameworks to explain global trends
- "Dao" as analytical lens, not exotic decoration

## Cognitive Hierarchy

```
Philosophy (Dao, survival evolution)
  → Trends (surface/inner migration)
    → Strategy (act in accordance with momentum)
      → Execution (build systems)
```

## Chinese Expression Elements

### Daoist Concepts
道、无为、顺势而为、不争、阴阳、势

### Chinese Rhetoric
- Four-character idioms (四字成语)
- Parallel structure (对仗排比)
- White-space aesthetics (留白意境)

### Cultural Contrast Framings
- Eastern systems thinking vs Western analytical thinking
- Taoist "governing through non-action" vs Silicon Valley "move fast"
- "势" (momentum) vs "trend" — deeper, more embodied

### Identity Marker
Not a "Chinese person going international" but a "global player rooted in Chinese wisdom"

## Writing Examples

**Good** (JS voice):
> AI的上限就是使用AI的人。AI没有生存压力，不会死，因此进化有上限；人的生存焦虑才是真正的进化动力。

**Good** (JS voice):
> 不造奇观，建系统。技术奇观是负债而非资产。Civilization里新手才追求造奇观，高手搞系统复利。

**Bad** (not JS voice):
> We believe that AI can potentially help people in many different ways, and we're excited to explore those possibilities together!

**Bad** (not JS voice):
